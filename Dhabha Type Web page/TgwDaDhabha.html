Gulab Jamun

Gulab jamun is a sweet confectionery or dessert, originating in the Indian subcontinent, and a type of mithai popular in India, Bangladesh, Nepal, Pakistan, the Maldives, as well as Myanmar

Ingredients

  • Oil
  • Bread
  • Sugar
  • Milk Powder
  • Flour
  • Yogurt

Process

  1. Prepare the Dough
  2. Shape The Jamuns
  3. Deep-frying balls
  4. Prepare the Syrup
  5. Soak the Jamuns
  6. Serve

Barfi

Barfi or burfi is a milk-based sweet from the Indian subcontinent with a fudge-like consistency. It is consumed throughout India and Pakistan and is especially popular in North India. Barfi is often served at celebrations and religious festivals such as Diwali and Holi.

Ingredients

  • Oil
  • Condensed milk
  • Sugar
  • Milk Powder
  • Flour

Process



  1. Prepare the Dough
  2. Shape The barfi
  3. Add the Syrup
  4. Soak the Barfi
  5. Serve

Gajar ka Halwa

Gajar ka halwa, also known as gajorer halwa, gajarno halwo, gajrela, gajar pak, and carrot pudding, is a sweet Indian dessert made by placing grated carrots in a pot containing a specific amount of water, milk, sugar, and cardamom and then cooking with ghee while stirring regularly.

Ingredients

  • Oil
  • Condensed milk
  • Sugar
  • Milk Powder
  • Gajar

Process



  1. Prepare the Milk
  2. Cut The Gajar
  3. Add Sugar
  4. Cook it
  5. Serve
